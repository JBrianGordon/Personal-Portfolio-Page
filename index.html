GoturBAC

Phone/Web App

GoturBAC is web app I designed to give an estimate for a users BAC level, based on a BAC calculator I built in Ruby. I created it in anticipation of a graduation party, to help people have an idea of just how intoxicated they may be.

One feature I decided to add was the "Preferred BAC" setting. The user, once they hit a point where they feel good but haven't gone overboard, can set a preferred BAC that they can refer to in the future, so they'll know their sweet spot on just how much is enough. Nobody likes to go overboard.

For this app I wanted to create a relational model between the users and the drinks, and hand roll my own authentication. I used RoR for the backend, and PostgreSQL for the database. Most of the styling is done using Zurb Foundation. It's best to check this one out on your phone, but the styling is responsive to all screens.

WhAttire

Web App

WhAttire was a group project I did with some of my very talented classmates in General Assembly.

Our idea was to create an app similar to Yelp, but with a focus on proper attire and dress codes. It can be difficult to tell what is appropriate to wear to a venue you've never been to, and it's embarassing to be over or underdressed. Yelp doesn't seem to provide much in the way of answers, and even when it does give a dress code, it's usually pretty vague. WhAttire is the solution to that problem.

My main focus on this project was to handle a lot of the frontend. In particular I'm proud of the splashdown page, which was my first attempt at parallax scrolling. This was also a great experience in learning how to work as a team over Github, and I got tons of experience with branching and pull requests from this project.

CineSync

Web App

CineSync is my first real Web App, and it's one that I'd still like to put a lot more work into. The internet already has a few review amalgamation sites like Rotten Tomatoes and Metacritic, but sometimes even these sites aren't terribly helpful. Maybe you hate the movies critics tend to clamor over, or maybe you like horror movies but they always seem to get bad reviews.

The idea for CineSync is that instead of searching through critics reviews, you can find users whose taste in movies align more with your own. This would make it easier to find new movies that you'll love, and avoid movies that you'd hate. You can follow users and read reviews that appeal to who you are as a movie goer, and even write your own and develop a following of like minded cinephiles.

CineSync was my first use of an API, in this case the Rotten Tomatoes API. The styling is done using Bootstrap 3, and the backend is Ruby on Rails. For the database, I used Mongo.

Tic Tac Toe

Game

For our first project at General Assembly, we were instructed to create a game of Tic Tac Toe. Other than creating a game that followed the basic rules, we could design the game any way we wanted to, primarily using JavaScript (Angular JS), and CSS.

For my version, I wanted to create something both fun and kind of weird. I went with a "Bateman vs. Batman" theme, and added some celebration gifs at the end depending on the winner. This was a fun way to learn JS and CSS, along with game logic.
